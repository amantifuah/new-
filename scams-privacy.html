Protect yourself
from online threats

What are scams?

Scams are illegal, deceptive schemes where criminals trick people into giving away money, personal information, or valuable data by creating fake, urgent, or too-good-to-be-true situations, often impersonating legitimate organizations or using fear tactics to pressure victims into acting quickly. They exploit trust and emotion, using technology like fake emails (phishing), texts, or phone calls, and can range from simple confidence tricks to sophisticated operations.

How people get tricked

Through
Impersonation:Typically, scammers pretend to be from a well-known trusted business, government department or organisation and they threaten you into handing over your money or personal details.
Phishing: is a way that cybercriminals steal confidential information such as online banking logins, credit card details, business login credentials or passwords/passphrases. They do this by sending fraudulent messages and emails (sometimes called ‘lures’).
Charity:Some scammers will contact you via phone, mail, email or face-to-face and pretend to be a charity. Often these messages will look like they real thing, but then they will ask you to click on a link, make a payment or provide personal information.

Why you must protect your personal information

You should protect your personal information to prevent identity theft, financial fraud, reputational damage, and loss of privacy, which can lead to significant financial ruin, emotional distress, and long-term legal issues, ultimately giving you control over your digital life and safeguarding your personal boundaries. It's crucial because your data builds a digital picture of you that criminals can exploit for unauthorized access, illegal activities, and personal gain, impacting your security and autonomy.

How to stay safe

Nobody is immune from scams. The criminals behind it target people online and in their homes, often emotionally manipulating their victims before they steal money or personal data. But there is something we can do. By staying vigilant and always taking a moment to stop, think and check whenever we’re approached, we can help to protect ourselves and each other from scams.
